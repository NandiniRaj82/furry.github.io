WHY NOT STREET DOGS ?
Potential adopters may fear dogs, but adopting one can help overcome that fear.
Myths about street dog

All street dogs are aggressive:

While some street dogs may display aggression due to fear or past experiences,most are friendly and approachable. They often learn to coexist peacefully with humans and other animals in their environment.

Street dogs are all unhealthy:

hile street dogs may face health challenges such as parasites or injuries, many are surprisingly resilient and can adapt to their environment. With proper care and attention, they can lead healthy lives.

Street dogs cannot be trained:

Street dogs are intelligent and can be trained just like any other dog. While they may require more patience and understanding due to their backgrounds, they can learn obedience and even perform tricks with consistent training.

All street dogs are stray:

Not all dogs found on the streets are without owners. Some may have wandered off or been abandoned, but others may have caretakers within the community who provide them with food and shelter.

Street dogs are all the same breed:

Street dogs come from a variety of breeds and mixes, and their appearance can vary greatly. They are often a blend of different breeds, making each one unique.

Street dogs cannot be adopted:

Many street dogs are successfully adopted into loving homes. Animal shelters and rescue organizations work to rehabilitate street dogs and find them suitable forever homes where they can thrive.
